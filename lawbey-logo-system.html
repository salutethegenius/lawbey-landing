The Statute Lines Mark

Six horizontal lines representing lines of legal text — a statute, a judgment, a contract. Five lines sit at reduced opacity. The third line is amber, extended, terminated with a filled circle. That is the retrieved law.

The retrieval cursor (the circle) is the only geometric element in the system. It marks the moment the AI finds the answer. Everything else is context.

  • Lines 1,2,4,5,6: 15% opacity — the unread corpus
  • Line 3: Amber, 100% — the retrieved statute
  • Cursor: filled circle + halo ring at line terminus
  • Wordmark: Cormorant 600 "Law" + italic 400 "Bey"
  • Sub: DM Mono, all caps, tracking +2px
  • "Bey" italic signals accessibility, not formality

The Pillar Arc

A single courthouse column — base, steps, shaft, capital — abstracted to rectangles. Above it, a semicircular arc opens outward, terminating in tick marks and a central apex dot.

The column is the law, fixed and immovable. The arc is access — knowledge radiating outward. Together they say: the law was always there. LawBey opens it up.

  • Column: 4 stacked rects, no curves — architectural
  • Arc: SVG path, 30px radius from column top
  • Three tick marks: left, apex, right
  • Apex dot: amber filled circle — the answer
  • Best for: legal aid, education, print, signage

LB Monogram

L is constructed as a bracket — vertical spine plus right-angle foot. It reads as the letter, as a reference bracket [, and as an open door. B is two document pages sharing a left spine, representing indexed sources.

Amber lines inside each B page are the retrieved content. At 16px they vanish and only the structural letterforms remain — which is the correct behaviour.

  • L: 2 rects, right-angle only — no diagonal
  • B: shared spine + 2 outlined page rects
  • Amber text lines: 2 per page, drop at 24px
  • At 16px: L + B outlines only, still readable
  • Statute Mark reads better at 16px — test both

Amber Protocol — The Retrieval Colour

Amber appears only on: the retrieved line in the mark, "Bey" in the wordmark, the cursor circle, the Pro badge, and thin amber rules that divide sections. It does not appear on body text or navigation.

Amber communicates truth surfaced from text. It is not decorative. Every amber element in the system answers the same question: "what did the retrieval find?" Keep it sparse. Sparse amber earns trust.
